Dayside

Before you continue

Please read this carefully. You can decline at any time — nothing is scanned or transmitted unless you agree.

What Dayside does when you click "Start scan"

Dayside looks at the following on this computer only, when you explicitly start a scan:

  • Processes currently running and their names
  • Visible application windows and their titles
  • Open browser tabs (titles, plus URLs on macOS)
  • Connected audio and video devices
  • System state: monitor count, remote-session status, architecture

It compares this information against a list of known AI interview assistants and suspicious patterns. Findings appear on your screen for your review.

Nothing happens automatically

Agreeing to this consent does not start a scan. It opens the main Dayside window in a "Ready" state. You choose when to run the first scan by clicking the Start scan button — typically when your interview begins and you're sharing your screen. You are in control of the timing.

Optional: continuous checking during the interview

In the main window you'll see a checkbox labeled "Keep checking every 5 minutes". This is off by default. If you enable it, Dayside will re-run the same scan every 5 minutes to catch anything launched after the interview starts.

You can turn it on, turn it off, or leave it off entirely. This decision is yours.

Compact mode (always-on-top pill)

Dayside can shrink to a small, draggable status pill that stays on top of other windows. This lets the interviewer see at a glance that the scan is current and the machine is clean, without the full window blocking your work. You can toggle between full and compact at any time.

Dayside does not hide itself from screen capture. It is designed to be visible to both you and the interviewer during the screen-share. That visibility is the point of the tool.

Dayside is read-only

Dayside only observes. It does not close, terminate, modify, or change anything on your computer. If a flagged item turns out to be something you want to close, you do that yourself using Task Manager (Windows) or Activity Monitor (macOS) — the same way you'd close any other program. Dayside never touches your machine; it only shows you what's there.

What Dayside does not do

  • No upload. No network calls. No server communication. Ever.
  • No recording. Screen, camera, and microphone are not accessed.
  • No biometrics. No face scan, voice print, or keystroke capture.
  • No persistent data. When this app closes, nothing remains.
  • No changes to your system. The tool cannot close, start, or modify any process, file, or setting.
  • No file contents. Your documents, emails, messages, and personal files are not read.
  • No hiding. Dayside itself never disappears from the screen-share. Compact mode makes it smaller, not invisible.

Your responsibility

You are running this tool voluntarily on your own computer. You alone decide whether to run it, when to start the scan, whether to enable periodic re-scans, what results to share, and what (if anything) to do about what it shows.

Interview context

If you are running this as part of a job interview, the employer conducting that interview is responsible for lawful use — including accommodations, privacy notices, and applicable employment laws in their jurisdiction. This tool is a diagnostic, not a hiring decision. Any decision based on what it shows is the employer's, not this project's.

Accessibility

If you rely on assistive software (screen readers, voice-control, captioning, magnification, etc.), tell your interviewer before the scan. These tools may appear in the results and benefit from context you can provide. Dayside maintains an allowlist of common assistive tools — if something you use is flagged incorrectly, that's a bug we want to fix.

Accuracy

Detection is not perfect. Legitimate software can be flagged by mistake, and some AI assistants may go undetected. Treat every flag as a signal for discussion, not a verdict.
